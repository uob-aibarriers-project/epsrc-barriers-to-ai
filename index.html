What We Did

Our research utilized a robust mixed-methods approach to map the transition from undergraduate to doctoral AI studies. We developed a custom Natural Language Processing (NLP) pipeline to analyze over 2,600 PhD recruitment listings, identifying linguistic and structural barriers.

Parallel to this, we collaborated with the Jean Golding Institute (JGI) to deliver workshops designed for students from non-technical backgrounds, tracking shifts in self-efficacy and study intentions.

  • 03

Why It Matters

Economic Innovation

The AI talent shortage is a bottleneck for innovation. By expanding the pipeline, we ensure the most capable minds shape the next era.

Algorithmic Justice

Inclusion is critical for preventing bias. Diverse researchers bring perspectives necessary for fair, representative systems.

Institutional Reform

Findings reveal "False Hope" barriers. We provide evidence to help universities reform recruitment pipelines.
