Mixed-Methods Data Insights

Key Research Findings

Evidence of structural "False Hope" in recruitment, measurable literacy gains through targeted training, and deep-seated confidence barriers among non-STEM disciplines.

1. Structural Barriers: The "False Hope" Gap

Our NLP analysis of 2,643 active PhD listings (FindAPhD.com, Dec 2025) identified a major structural discrepancy between institutional branding and funding reality.

Global Ambition vs. National Restriction

"While 79.2% of funded projects advertise themselves as available worldwide, this contrasts with the typical 30% international recruitment cap in UKRI-funded cohorts, creating a 'False Hope' barrier for global talent."

2. Impact on AI Literacy

+16.2%

Knowledge Acquisition

Mean raw AI literacy scores shifted from 52.3% (pre) to 68.5% (post) following our introductory intervention.

+21.7pp

STEM Catalyst Effect

STEM students saw significantly higher growth, effectively widening the literacy gap compared to non-STEM peers (+11.4pp).

0.5 SD

IRT Ability Shift

The Item Response Theory (IRT) model showed a positive shift of 0.5 standard deviations in "true ability" estimates.

3. Qualitative Dimensions

Theme: Technical Gatekeeping

"I never got any chance to study Python... it seems so complicated to a person from a social science major."

Prior experiences in high school and undergraduate choices act as long-term filters. However, training shifted perceptions from AI as a "math-only" field to an accessible tool.

Theme: Career Expectations

"I chose my degree because it's very broad... you always need a mechanical engineer. For AI, job opportunities seem limited."

Ambiguity about post-doctoral career paths outside of "Big Tech" hubs remains a significant barrier for students evaluating long-term research commitment.

4. Perception of Institutional Policy

Clarity as an Enabler

Participants found policy examples (listing limits of use) useful. Familiarity with institutional guidance correlates with higher confidence in using AI ethically.

The Constraint Effect

Some participants report feeling "limited" by policy even for personal work, raising suspicions about long-term professional autonomy in AI.

Language Equity

International students highlighted that policy flexibility regarding AI for translation/grammar check is a critical enabler for equity.
