Deep Data Analysis

Mapping the Evidence

From institutional market analysis of 2,382 listings to quantifying the impact of AI literacy training. Our data reveals a landscape of structural ambiguity and significant potential for growth.

1. Structural & Financial Landscapes

Analysis of recruitment data shows that PhD funding is uneven and uncertain. While many projects are "funded," the mechanisms often exclude those without prior capital.

"The financial burden remains a substantial barrier to entry, particularly in highly competitive and resource-intensive fields like AI."

Figure 1: Breakdown of funding status across UK AI PhD listings.

1.1 The Funding Split

Only 48.6% of listings provide direct project funding. A significant 21.5% require students to enter university competitions, introducing a layer of risk and delay for the applicant.

1.2 Stipend Benchmarks

Adverts heavily cluster around the UKRI minimum of £20,780, with predictable adjustments for London-based institutions.

1.3 The Top-Up Gap

Only 30.3% of listings mention additional funding for professional development, leaving 70% of students without clear support for international conferences.

1.4 Institutional Disparities

The availability of research support varies wildly. Bristol sits in the middle tier (~22%), while Exeter leads with over 60%.

1.5 Ambiguity as a Filter

15.8% of listings are vaguely worded. This ambiguity deters applicants who lack institutional insider knowledge.

2. Academic Gatekeeping & Symbols

Is AI an institutionally closed domain? Our analysis shows a shift from rigid grades to "implicit" technical filters and a visible gender void in leadership.

2.1 Softening Grade Pressure

Increasing acceptance of 2:1 degrees suggests a move away from extreme academic exceptionalism at the entry level.

2.2 The Professional Gap

Only 34.2% of listings explicitly value prior professional experience, reinforcing academic pathways as the primary route.

2.3 The Paywall Trap

Structural Misalignment

95% of listings are open to global talent, but few cover the ~£15k fee shortfall for international students.

2.4 Networking Hurdle

Social Capital

Mandating pre-application contact with supervisors acts as a barrier for those without established academic networks.

2.5 Passive Inclusion

EDI Framing

Diversity statements are overwhelmingly passive "encouragements" rather than actionable support frameworks.

The Visible "Gender Void"

Our analysis of 1,065 UK AI PhD ads confirms that supervisory leadership remains overwhelmingly male-dominated. This lack of visible role models significantly lowers AI self-efficacy for female candidates.

Indicator: 0% explicit mention of neurodiversity support.
